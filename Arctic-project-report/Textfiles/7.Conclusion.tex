\section{Conclusion}
To conclude, the Arctic climate is a complex and dynamic system that is influenced by both the atmospheric circulation, the cryosphere and the ocean. 
Both the polar cell and the global meridional overturning circulation, have a direct impact on the Arctic climate, leading to a dry desert-like climate. Due to the forming and melting of sea ice in the cryosphere, brine rejection makes the cooling water from the Atlantic sink and return as cold bottom water. The Arctic is also experiencing a phenomenon called Arctic amplification, which is the enhanced global warming  compared to the rest of the world. This has had direct impacts on the societal activities in the region, such as the opening of new shipping routes and resource extraction due to the retreat of the ice cap, making the region interesting for major powers in the region. The Arctic is thus a region with growing geopolitical interest, which may have significant impacts on the people living in the Arctic, as well as the environment in the region.
